# Maldives-Analysis

Collection of data-driven analyses of the Maldives economic crisis using verified Maldives Monetary Authority (MMA) data, IMF assessments, and Moody's credit analysis.

## Articles

### 1. **Debt Trap: The Unraveling of Maldives' Economic Miracle**
[Read Article →](Debt Trap.html)

**Topic:** Fiscal & Debt Crisis  
**Key Finding:** Persistent structural deficits of 11–13% of GDP (2020–2025) have doubled debt while government revenue growth collapsed  

**What You'll Learn:**
- Why the fiscal deficit is structural, not cyclical
- How 52.3B MVR in short-term T-bills create refinancing trap
- Why revenue growth (2.7%) lags GDP growth (5%)
- Timeline: When IMF program becomes forced (2026–2027)

**For:** Economists, policymakers, fiscal reform advocates

### 2. **The Tourism Plantation Economy: Why More Tourists = Less Money for Maldivians**
[Read Article →](Tourism Plantation Economy.html)

**Topic:** Tourism Value Capture  
**Key Finding:** 75% tourist growth brought almost no benefit to government because 75% of tourism is foreign-owned with profits extracted overseas

**What You'll Learn:**
- Why more tourists doesn't mean more government revenue
- How foreign ownership captures 70% of resort profits
- Why average tourist stay is declining (7.6 → 6.1 days)
- Why tourism tax revenue is only 2% of government revenue despite being 60% of exports
- How Maldives operates as a "plantation economy"

**For:** Tourism economists, development researchers, policy critics

## Key Data Sources

All analysis based on verified government statistics:

- **Maldives Monetary Authority (MMA)** - Primary source for all economic data
  - Tables 2.1–3.3: GDP & Tourism
  - Tables 6.1–6.6: Fiscal & Debt
  - Table 13.1: Balance of Payments

- **IMF Article IV Consultations** (2024, 2025) - Official assessments
  - Fiscal deficit estimates
  - Debt sustainability analysis
  - Risk ratings: "High risk of debt distress"

- **Moody's Credit Ratings** (September 2024)
  - Sovereign rating: Caa2 (speculative grade)
  - Key concern: Default risks rising
